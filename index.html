Show information about Evangelista Torricelli
The famous Italian physicist and mathematician is the
of the barometer scientific tool used in the field of
meteorology to estimate atmospheric pressure, built
in 1643. It would be interesting note that a number
of Italian Navy submarines were named after
the inventor.






Show information about Ferdinand Verbiest
Verbiest was an astronomer and a mathematician.
He was the one to invent the world's first
automobile. The inventor came up with the idea
to create an automobile while visiting China as
a missionary. His automobile was powered
by steam, but could not carry humans.





Show information about Charles Babbage
Charles Babbage was an English mathematician, philosopher,
inventor and mechanical engineer who originated
the concept of a programmable computer.
Considered as “Father of Computers”, Babbage is
credited with inventing the first mechanical computer
that eventually led to more complex designs.





Show information about Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen
The famous German physicist Röntgen is the one who
discovered the X-rays (also known as Röntgen rays).
This invention allowed the German scientist
win the first Nobel Prize in Physics in 1901.





Show information about Thomas Edison

He has made a large number of inventions,
but the most wellknown one is the electric bulb.
Among other discoveries of Thomas Edison
there are telegraph devices, phonograph, carbon transmitter,
direct current generator, universal electric motor, and more.





Show information about Emile Berliner

The German-born Jewish American scientist became known for
his disc record gramophone (in the United States known as
phonograph or record player). Used for recording and
reproducing sounds on a gramophone record, vinyl record, the
device (with certain modifications made once in a while) was
popular until 1980s.





Show information about Alexander Graham Bell

During the experiments he carried out with the telegraph,
Bell came up with the idea of the telephone.
The inventor of one of the most popular devices today
thought that the telephone was intruding, that is why he did
not have one in his workplace.





Show information about Rudolf Christian Karl Diesel

Being a mechanical engineer, Rudolf Christian Karl Diesel
managed to discover the diesel engine.
The German inventor was also a well-known thermal engineer, a
polyglot, an expert in arts, and a social theorist.





Show information about Albert Einstein

One of the greatest scientists of the 20th century is the creator
of numerous inventions and theories that transformed a lot of
concepts linked to space and time, with the most important
discovery being the theory of relativity. Other discoveries of
Einstein include the photoelectric effect and the Einstein calculator.
